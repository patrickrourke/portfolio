About Me

I graduated from Brown University in May 2025 with degrees in Education Studies and Social Analysis and Research. I am intellectually curious and have a passion for exploring policy areas across the social sciences. Whether it was studying contraceptive use by college students in Zambia as a Research Fellow at the Annenberg Institute, investigating public school funding inequities as a Research Assistant for the "Hidden Funds" project, or collecting data on gender- and race-based occupational attainment gaps in my current role as a Research Associate for Demographic Perspectives, I actively seek out opportunities to use my data, organization, and research skills for projects that support the common good. I am eager to use my blend of quantitative and qualitative research skills to contribute to research projects that inform policy and practice across the social sciences.
