Research & Projects

Geospatial Analysis

I extracted school district border raster data from the Stanford Education Data Archive, student test score data from the state education departments of Mississippi and Washington, and internet accessibility data from the U.S. Census Bureau to study the effect of the digital divide on test scores during the COVID-19 pandemic. I created maps using ArcGIS and performed geospatial analysis using GeoDa. You can zoom in on the poster for a closer look!

Education Capstone

I used a mixed-methods approach to investigate the benefits of peer advising structures in a university setting. I conducted a literature review on previous research and surveyed and interviewed peer advisors at Brown University. I synthesized qualitative data and identified key themes that highlighted the benefits of peer advising as an opportunity to increase belonging and retention among first-year college students and provide a valuable professional development opportunity for upperclassmen.

Skills

  • Mixed Methods Research
  • Literature Review
  • Interviewing
  • Survey Design
  • Qualitative Data Analysis

Teaching

I have had the opportunity to teach in many capacities. I have served in public elementary schools as a paraeducator and substitute teacher for grades Pre-K through sixth and “specials” classrooms, such as PE, art, music, and Spanish. I was a teaching assistant at Brown University for two years where I facilitated discussion groups and leveraged my position as a Leader in the Meiklejohn Peer Advising Program to train and mentor the next generation of peer advisors.
